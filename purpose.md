# Purpose

This file states **why** this second brain exists. `AGENTS.md` and skills define how agents operate; this page defines strategic direction so ingest, query, review, crystallization, and research planning can prioritize what matters.

## Mission

Turn raw material and exploratory sessions into durable, compounding memory that can be reused for future decisions, writing, research, and self-understanding.

## Current scope

Priority growth areas:

1. Projects and work
2. Research and learning
3. Ideas and writing
4. People and relationships
5. Health, habits, and personal systems

## Key questions to refine

- Which projects or life areas deserve the most knowledge-compounding effort right now?
- Which recurring questions should this wiki answer better over time?
- Which sources are highest leverage to ingest next?
- Which outputs should become canonical wiki knowledge rather than remaining one-off analyses?
- Which personal or sensitive areas require stricter visibility boundaries?

## Active themes

- Build a manual-first, human-steered LLM wiki that can later support hooks and scheduled workflows.
- Prefer evidence-backed semantic knowledge over isolated summaries.
- Convert useful exploration into crystallizations, then promote stable lessons into the wiki.
- Maintain browsability in Obsidian through links, Bases, Canvases, and concise index/overview pages.

## Promotion preferences

Promote content upward when it is:

- likely to be reused
- supported by sources or repeated sessions
- connected to existing wiki pages
- relevant to active questions or priority growth areas
- safe to preserve at the chosen visibility level

Keep content in raw/outputs rather than canonical wiki when it is:

- transient, speculative, or weakly supported
- mostly a record of one session
- sensitive enough to require narrow handling
- not yet connected to a durable concept, project, person, or decision

## Out of scope / caution

- Do not pretend automation exists when workflows are manual.
- Do not add graph/database/search infrastructure claims unless the repo actually has them.
- Do not preserve secrets, credentials, or unsafe private details in downstream wiki/output artifacts.
